## `> whoami`

<img align="right" width="36%" src="https://github-readme-stats.vercel.app/api?username=YOUR_USERNAME&show_icons=true&theme=github_dark&hide_border=true&title_color=58a6ff&icon_color=58a6ff&text_color=8b949e&bg_color=0d1117&hide_title=true&rank_icon=github"/>

**People leave signals everywhere.**

A scroll that paused. A purchase that almost didn't happen. A feature nobody opens.

Underneath all of it — stories about how people actually make decisions.

That's what pulled me into data science. Not the models. Not the math. Not the tools. The stubborn belief that human behavior, if you look long enough, starts to make a strange kind of sense.

I notice which chair a stranger picks in an empty café. Which word makes someone stop scrolling. Which moment a conversation quietly shifts — and why. Most people call it overthinking. I call it the only thinking that ever felt natural.

My process starts before the dataset.

```
  understand the business  →  understand the people  →  ask the right question
                                                               ↑
                                                    get this wrong, nothing else matters
```

## `> cat future_directions.txt`

<br/>

Right now I'm most drawn to where **behavioral patterns and AI start to overlap** — how products can stop making educated guesses about people and start actually reflecting who they are.

The problems I want to work on:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  How do you build recommendation systems that understand intent,         │
│  not just history?                                                       │
│                                                                         │
│  How do you design AI products that adapt to how a person thinks,       │
│  not how a dataset predicts they'll behave?                             │
│                                                                         │
│  What does responsible personalization look like at scale?              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

I don't have the answers yet. But I have the questions, and I've learned that's the harder part.

> *Still early in the journey. Direction matters more than distance.*
