**Which chair a stranger picks in an empty café.**  
**Which word in a sentence makes someone stop scrolling.**  
**Which moment a conversation quietly shifts — and why.**

Most people call it overthinking.  
I call it the only thinking that ever felt natural to me.

People leave signals everywhere. A scroll that paused. A purchase that almost didn't happen. A feature nobody opens. Underneath all of it — stories about how people actually make decisions.

That's what pulled me into data analytics. Not the models. Not the math. Not the tools. The stubborn belief that human behavior, if you look long enough, starts to make a strange kind of sense.

## My Process

> *Get the question wrong — nothing else matters.*

```
Before the dataset:      Understand the business.
Before the business:     Understand the people.
Before the people:       Ask the right question.
```

That discipline didn't come from classrooms.

- **Hackathons** — where ambiguity is the default and shipping something real is the only goal
- **LeetCode** — not for streaks, but because clean thinking under constraints is a muscle, and muscles need reps
- **Teaching at Stanford CIP** — where you discover fast whether you truly understand something, or just think you do

Each one taught the same thing differently: **pressure reveals clarity.**
