People leave signals everywhere.

A scroll that paused. A purchase that almost didn't happen. A feature nobody opens.

Underneath all of it — stories about how people actually make decisions. That's what pulled me into data science. Not the models. Not the math. Not the tools. The belief that human behavior, if you look long enough, starts to make a strange kind of sense.

My process starts before the dataset. Understand the business. Understand the people. Then ask the right question — because get that wrong, and nothing else matters.

## Where this is heading

<br/>

Right now I'm most drawn to where behavioral patterns and AI start to overlap — how products can stop making educated guesses about people and start actually reflecting who they are.

The questions I keep coming back to: How do you build systems that understand intent, not just history? What does responsible personalization look like at scale? How do you design AI products that adapt to how a person thinks, not how a dataset predicts they'll behave?

I don't have the answers yet. I have the questions. I've learned that's the harder part.
